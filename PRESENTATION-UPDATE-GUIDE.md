### Slide 5: Surface Analysis
**What to say:**
- "The surface analysis shows high pressure over [location]"
- "A cold front is positioned from [A] to [B]"
- "The pressure gradient between [high] and [low] is driving strong winds over New England"
- "Concord is currently in [post-frontal/pre-frontal/warm sector] conditions"

**Key Features to Identify:**
- High pressure centers (H) and values
- Low pressure centers (L) and values
- Front locations (cold, warm, stationary, occluded)
- Isobars - tight = windy!

### Slide 8: 300mb Jet Stream
**What to say:**

- Right Entrance: Subsidence (sinking)
- Left Exit: Rising
- Right Exit: Rising
- Left Entrance: Subsidence (sinking)

## DYNAMICAL REASONING PHRASES TO USE

### For Upper-Level Support:
- "Positive vorticity advection ahead of the shortwave trough provides upward vertical motion"
- "The jet streak's right-exit quadrant is positioned over [region], providing upper-level divergence"
- "Strong isentropic lift as warm, moist air rides up and over the cold air mass"
- "Height falls at 500mb indicate a deepening trough"
- "The omega equation suggests strong ascent due to [vorticity advection/thermal advection]"

### For Temperature:
- "Cold air advection behind the front will drop temperatures below normal"
- "Warm air advection ahead of the front will boost temperatures"
- "Strong subsidence behind the high pressure will create a temperature inversion"
- "Diabatic cooling from evaporation will limit daytime heating"
- "Clear skies overnight will allow strong radiational cooling"

### For Precipitation:
- "Isentropic lift along the warm front will produce steady light to moderate rain"
- "Frontogenetic forcing will enhance precipitation rates along the frontal boundary"
- "Low-level jet at 850mb transporting [X] g/kg of moisture"
- "Lift mechanism includes: [frontal forcing/isentropic lift/orographic enhancement/jet dynamics]"
- "The NAM shows stronger QPF due to better resolution of mesoscale precipitation bands"

### For Winds:
- "Tight pressure gradient with [X] millibars over [distance] will support winds of [Y] mph"
- "Strong winds aloft at 850mb ([X] knots) will mix down to the surface with high mixing heights"
- "Ageostrophic winds in the exit region of the jet streak will enhance surface winds"

## COMMON QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

**Q: "Why did you choose [GFS/NAM] over the other model?"**
A: "I weighted the [chosen model] more heavily because [1] the ensemble members support this solution, [2] recent verification shows better performance, and [3] it makes physical sense given the synoptic pattern."

**Q: "What if the low tracks 50 miles further south?"**
A: "A southern track would mean [colder temperatures/less precipitation/snow instead of rain] because [Concord would be in the cold sector/less moisture transport/etc.]"

**Q: "How confident are you in this forecast?"**
A: "I have high confidence in the general pattern - [X]. However, there's lower confidence in [Y] because [model spread/ensemble uncertainty/mesoscale details]."

**Q: "Could we see thunderstorms?"**
A: "Thunderstorms are [unlikely/possible] because [CAPE values are X/shear is Y/instability is Z]."

**Q: "What about snow?"**
A: "[Snow is not expected at this time/Snow is possible in higher elevations] because [temperatures/elevation/cold air depth]."
